What is Stage 3 Chronic Kidney Disease?

Stage 3 CKD means your kidneys are moderately damaged and not filtering waste as well as they should. Your eGFR (estimated glomerular filtration rate) is between 30-59 mL/min/1.73m¬≤.

Why is Diet Important?

A proper diet can:

  • Slow disease progression
  • Protect remaining kidney function
  • Manage blood pressure
  • Prevent complications
  • Improve overall health and quality of life

Key Nutrients to Monitor:

Nutrient Why Limit? Recommended Daily Limit
Sodium (Salt) Increases blood pressure, strains kidneys < 2,300 mg per day
Potassium Can build up to dangerous levels 2,000-3,000 mg per day (varies)*
Phosphorus Damages bones and blood vessels 800-1,000 mg per day (varies)*
Protein Creates waste kidneys must filter 0.6-0.8g per kg body weight*

*Individual needs vary. Always follow your doctor's and renal dietitian's specific recommendations.
